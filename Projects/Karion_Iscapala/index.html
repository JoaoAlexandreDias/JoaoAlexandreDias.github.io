Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala

This tiny species of fish comes in all sorts of colors—and, for some strange reason, a variety of expressions.

No bigger than a thumb, it can barely swim against Karion’s ocean currents, leading researchers to believe it
absorbs nutrients directly from the water.

Tools Used:

  • Adobe Photoshop

Happy Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala charoumena

Green and very cheerful, this variant is friendly and, as long as it isn't harmed, doesn’t seem to mind the presence of researchers.

Surprised Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala ecplictos

White and perpetually surprised, this variant is a bit more cautious of its surroundings. Researchers observed that it will attempt to fit almost anything into its mouth.

Scared Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala fovismena

The purple variant is the most skittish of them all, often trying to flee or hide when observed.

Sad Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala lypiros

The sad-looking variant is quite similar to the happy one in that it trusts researchers. However, it appears to “seek out” the company of other Iscapala more frequently than usual.

Angry Tiny Ball Fish

Iscapala thymoomenos

To complete the emotional spectrum, a red variant was discovered—far more aggressive than the rest. During observation, researchers recorded the following:

“It kept bumping into the aquarium wall while staring at me.”

They suspect this variant may lack a sense of self-preservation.
